Multi-Mass Tension Visualizer Guide

Physics Overview

This simulation visualizes a horizontal chain of connected masses experiencing tension under an applied force (F) pulling them to the right.

Static vs. Kinetic Friction:

Friction opposes the start of motion with static coefficient (μs) up to a threshold: fs,max = μs · Mtotal · g.

Once moving, dynamic motion is resisted by kinetic coefficient (μk): fk = μk · Mtotal · g.

The system will only accelerate if F > fs,max when static, transitioning to kinetic resistance when moving.

Newton's First Law (Velocity Persistence):

If the applied force balances out the friction force exactly (Fnet = 0), the system acceleration is a = 0. According to Newton's First Law, the system will continue sliding at its current constant velocity until external forces change.

Rope Tension Calculations:

Calculated at segment i pulling the masses behind it: Ti = (Σ M1..i) × aeffective

Where aeffective = (a + μk · g) when sliding, and F / Mtotal when static.

Controls & Customization

  • Mass Configuration: Adjust the sliders. Use the [+ Block] and [- Block] buttons to add or remove blocks from the workspace.
  • Dual Friction: Toggle friction on to reveal separate static and kinetic coefficient controllers.
  • Applied Force Knob: Drag circularly to apply tension. Ensure force overcomes static friction to begin sliding!
