Multi-Mass Tension Visualizer Guide

Physics Overview

This simulation visualizes a horizontal chain of four masses connected back-to-back by ropes, and pulled to the right by an applied force (F) on the front mass (M4).

System Acceleration (a):

a = Fnet / Mtotal

Where Fnet = F - Friction Force (if enabled), and Mtotal = M1 + M2 + M3 + M4

Rope Tension Calculations:
  • Tension 1 (T1) (between M1 & M2): pulls M1.
  • Tension 2 (T2) (between M2 & M3): pulls M1 and M2.
  • Tension 3 (T3) (between M3 & M4): pulls M1, M2, and M3.

Calculated as: Ti = (Σ M1..i) × aeffective

Controls & Customization

  • Mass Configuration: Drag the vertical sliders in the left panel to change individual masses. The blocks update in real time.
  • Applied Force Dial: Click and drag circularly (or use the fallback horizontal slider) to dynamically adjust the pulling force.
  • Ground Friction Toggle: Toggling this on simulates sliding kinetic friction (μk = 0.20), which opposes acceleration.
  • Simulation Play/Pause/Reset: Control the timeline. Speed buttons change the speed of animation and time integration.

Visual Annotations

  • Ropes change color intensity and thickness dynamically to reflect the magnitude of tension.
  • The Applied Force is visualized as a pulsating vector arrow pointing right from M4.
  • The live graph shows how tension scales along the position of the ropes, peaking at the front block.
